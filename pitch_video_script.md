# CoolBlock AI – Pitch Video Script (3-5 minutes)

## Video Structure & Timing

### Hook (0:00–0:30) - The Problem
**Script**:
"Last summer, a heatwave hit our city. The news said 'stay cool' – but where? Our neighborhoods don't have enough cooling centers, and residents don't know where to go. People end up in emergency rooms from heatstroke. We asked: what if there was an app that showed exactly where to find shade and AC?"

**Visual**: 
- Show news clips of heatwave or images of empty streets
- Show map with heat markers
- End with a student looking confused at their phone

---

### Solution (0:30–2:00) - Your AI Approach
**Script**:
"We built CoolBlock AI – an app that uses machine learning to map shade and cooling centers in your neighborhood.

Here's how it works: You open the app on your phone and type your address [show app opening]. The app pulls real-time temperature from weather data [show temperature display]. Then our AI model analyzes street images to find shaded areas [show model processing]. It combines both: high heat + no shade = red (high risk), green (low risk).

Click on a pin and get directions to the nearest library with AC or water fountain [show map interaction]. It's that simple."

**Visual**:
- Screen recording of the Streamlit app
- Show address input
- Show map loading and turning color-coded (green/yellow/red)
- Show clicking on a cooling center and getting directions
- Show temperature reading on map

---

### Demo (2:00–3:00) - Show It Working
**Script**:
"Let me show you the AI at work. This is a street with lots of trees and shade [upload image to model]. The model says '85% confidence: good shade.' [Show AI output]. Now look at this parking lot with no trees [upload second image]. The model says '92% confidence: no shade.' [Show output].

When we combine this with temperature – say it's 98 degrees – the map turns red in areas with no shade, warning residents to stay inside or head to a cooling center."

**Visual**:
- Upload street image to model → show shade score output
- Upload another image → show different shade score
- Show full map with color-coded risk zones
- Show user clicking through to get directions

---

### Impact (3:00–4:00) - Who Benefits?
**Script**:
"We tested this with 10 local high school students. One girl in a heat-vulnerable zip code didn't know there was a library with AC just 3 blocks from her house. She found it using our app. Another student realized they should check on elderly neighbors during heat waves.

This isn't just data on a map – it's a tool that helps people protect themselves and their communities during extreme heat."

**Visual**:
- Testimonial video clip (student saying what they learned)
- Or animated text showing use cases:
  - 👩‍🦱 "Found a cooling center I didn't know existed"
  - 🏠 "Checking on vulnerable neighbors"
  - 📍 "Planning my route to stay safe"

---

### Responsible AI & Next Steps (4:00–5:00)
**Script**:
"We designed this responsibly. Our AI isn't perfect – sometimes it's unsure about shade. When that happens, we show users a link to verify the location themselves using satellite view. And no matter what the AI says, we always show emergency numbers like 911 on the map.

Here's what's next: We want to add voice alerts in Spanish and other languages so everyone in our community can use it. We're also talking to local schools about distributing this before next summer's heatwave."

**Visual**:
- Show the "unsure shade" confidence threshold UI
- Show emergency number always displayed on map
- Show multilingual mockup (Spanish, etc.)
- Show partnership logos (local schools, nonprofits)
- End with CoolBlock AI logo and "Built with free tools. For community impact."

---

## Video Production Notes

### Tools to Use:
- **Screencast**: OBS Studio (free) or Loom (free, limited)
- **Editing**: iMovie (Mac), Windows Photos (Windows), or DaVinci Resolve (free)
- **Upload**: YouTube (unlisted) or Loom

### Tips:
- **Audio**: Use a quiet room, speak clearly, use a microphone if available
- **Pacing**: Don't rush – give people time to see the demo
- **Branding**: Add a simple intro slide with team name + logo
- **End Card**: "CoolBlock AI – AI for Heat Safety. Submit June 21, 2026"

### Length:
- Target: 3:45 – 4:30 minutes (within the 3–5 minute requirement)
- Practice once before recording to hit timing

---

## Key Messaging Checklist
- ✅ Hook grabs attention (relatable heat problem)
- ✅ Solution is clear (map + AI shade detection)
- ✅ Demo shows live product or prototype
- ✅ Impact story: real user benefited
- ✅ Responsible AI: explained safeguard
- ✅ Call to action: "Join the community"
